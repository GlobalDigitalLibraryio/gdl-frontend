Global digital library

The Global Digital Library (GDL) is intended to be a means of distributing mother tongue learning resources to support children’s literacy learning.

The GDL project is in the initial stages - developing a platform to ensure that high-quality mother tongue learning resources become available to all.

Early grade reading

Although there have been major strides in education over the past 15 years, 250 million children of primary school age are still unable to recognize basic letters and numbers.

The Global Digital Library is intended to be a means of distributing mother tongue learning resources worldwide to support children’s learning in their local languages.

Compilation of digital libraries

If you are looking for reading resources, All Children Reading have made a compilation of digital libraries, carefully curated to feature a variety of sites that provide local language early grade reading materials.

Project Partners

The Norwegian Agency for Development Cooperation and Norwegian Digital Learning Arena are leading the development of the Global Digital Library initiative as part of the Global Book Alliance. Other collaborating partners include the All Children Reading: A Grand Challenge for Development partners (USAID, World Vision, and the Australian Government) among other institutions.
